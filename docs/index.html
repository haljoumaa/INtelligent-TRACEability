Trace less. Type less.

INTRACE automates Digital Input (DI) extraction from BCU/LCC schematics. Calibration and final QA remain human. First-time projects typically complete in 30–90 minutes depending on layout variability and scan quality. Reuse is faster.

Download

What it does - and doesn’t

Does

Automates page/layout tagging, region cropping, OCR with rule checks, deterministic grouping, and export with anomaly flags.

Doesn’t

Remove calibration or human review. New vendors/layouts and poor scans still need setup and spot checks.

Typical matching

~95–100% per run (varies by vendor, layout variance, and scan quality). Worst-case datasets land around ~90% before review.

Why This Matters

Manual documentation from drawings is slow, inconsistent, and hard to audit. INTRACE reduces hands-on time, standardizes outputs, and preserves an audit trail so engineers focus on design, not transcription.

The old way

Manual tracing and data entry for 6–16 hours per unit; variable quality.

The INTRACE way

30–90 minutes for first-time projects; substantially faster with reuse.

Impact

90–98% time reduction, predictable scaling, and flagged anomalies for quick review.

How it works

1
Layout detection & tagging
Assign pages to vendor-specific layout IDs. Supports batch tagging and residue rules; saves for reuse.
2
Preprocessing & OCR
Normalize contrast, crop regions, and OCR both numbers and descriptions with rule-based validation.
3
Grouping & validation
Deterministic grouping, partial-group handling, cross-page consistency, and anomaly flags.
4
Export for QA review
Three Excel workbooks (Raw, Review, Final) plus JSON sidecars for metrics and OCR diagnostics.

Data handling & telemetry

Local-only logs

Logs stay on your machine. They include OCR diagnostics (engine/PSM, line/word confidence, whitelist “user‑words” hits) and application events (threading/pipeline stages).

Review workflow

Low-confidence items are flagged for quick human review; changes are tracked in a log.

Assumptions & limits

  • Clear scans (≥300 DPI) materially improve OCR stability.
  • Previously unseen layouts require a short calibration pass.
  • Heavily compressed PDFs, handwritten edits, or overlapping annotations reduce match rates.
